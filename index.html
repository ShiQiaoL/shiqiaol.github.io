Biography

I am currently a postdoctoral researcher at the National University of Singapore. My recent work focuses on fine-tuning universal machine-learned interatomic potentials and applying atomistic AI models to solid-state battery interfaces and energy conversion materials.

I received my PhD in Optical Engineering from Chongqing University in 2023. Across my projects, I am particularly interested in methods that connect rigorous atomistic physics with scalable, reproducible computational workflows.

Research Interests

Machine-learned interatomic potentials

Fine-tuning and evaluating universal MLIPs for reliable use in chemically complex materials systems.

Electrocatalysis and photocatalysis

Using first-principles calculations and data-driven screening to study activity, stability, and mechanism.

Battery materials and interfaces

Modeling ionic transport, interfacial stability, and structure-property relationships in next-generation batteries.
