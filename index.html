Research Themes

Method development and materials applications in one loop

My current work spans both methodological advances in machine-learned potentials and their deployment in realistic energy materials systems.

Foundation models for atomistic simulation

Fine-tuning and benchmarking universal machine-learned interatomic potentials such as MACE, NequIP, and CHGNet for reliable deployment across heterogeneous material systems.

Electrocatalysis and active-site design

Using first-principles calculations and AI-assisted screening to understand activity, selectivity, and stability in OER, ORR, and related energy-conversion reactions.

Battery interfaces and ionic transport

Modeling solid-state and interfacial phenomena in next-generation batteries, with an emphasis on transport mechanisms, degradation pathways, and structure-property relations.

Current Positioning

From physically grounded modeling to deployable AI workflows

I am particularly interested in turning recent atomistic foundation models into practical research tools for chemically complex materials, where transferability, long-timescale stability, and reproducibility all matter.
