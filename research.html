Research Portfolio

Research directions connecting atomistic detail with scalable discovery

The projects below reflect a common objective: making computational materials research faster, more transferable, and more physically trustworthy across complex energy-relevant systems.

01

Fine-tuning universal machine-learned interatomic potentials

I study how foundation atomistic models can be adapted to specific materials systems without losing robustness. This includes optimizer benchmarking, transferability analysis, data efficiency, and long-timescale molecular dynamics stability.

Representative methods: MACE, NequIP, CHGNet, PyTorch fine-tuning, uncertainty-aware validation.

02

Single-atom and multi-atom catalysts for energy conversion

I use density functional theory and AI-assisted workflows to probe adsorption energetics, spin states, reaction pathways, and structure-function relationships in catalytic systems relevant to OER, ORR, and electrochemical conversion.

Representative systems: C2N-supported transition metals, heterogeneous catalytic interfaces, reaction intermediates.

03

Battery interfaces and solid-state transport phenomena

My work explores how interfacial structure and local chemistry affect ionic transport, mechanical stability, and degradation in next-generation battery materials. I combine first-principles calculations with accelerated simulations to study interface evolution at scale.

Representative systems: Li-S batteries, solid-state electrolytes, heterointerfaces, defect-mediated transport.

04

High-throughput and reproducible computational workflows

Beyond individual projects, I care about workflow design: reproducible dataset preparation, HPC execution, evaluation pipelines, and visual communication that make complex atomistic studies easier to reuse and extend.

Representative tools: SLURM, Python automation, OVITO, VESTA, publication-quality scientific graphics.
